Blown to Bits Review

Book Authors: Hal Abelson, Ken Ledeen, Harry Lewis

"The Parking Garage Knows More than you Think" revolves on the "behind-the-scenes" technology. These are machines that work but isn't pubically announced. For example, some parking garages have visual detectors that match your lisence plate number with your ticket number. This is so people can't rob cars and pay their correct meter time.

"Connect the Dots" is about how different technologies can gather different amounts of information, usually meaningless, but when put together, can be the difference between freedom or jail time.

"Saving Time" focuses on the main reason why technology is so popular: it's a shortcut. Microwaves, cars, phones, etc. are all shortcuts for the goal we want to achieve.

My Interview

Interviewing my cousin Monica

Gabriel: How old were you when you got your first computer?

Monica: Probably like 16 when I came to the United States.

Gabriel: Growing up, what was your favorite piece of technology?

Monica: I loved cars, even when I was little I've wanted one.

Gabriel: How has technology changed over your lifetime?

Monica: Everything obviously got more advanced, phones got smaller and smarter and stuff like that.

Gabriel: Has the stuff you seen mostly hurt or help society?

Monica: I think technology has for the most part benefitted society. Everyone's connected now.

Gabriel: What is your greatest hope for future technology?

Monica: That it's like the movies, flying cars and sh*t.

Gabriel: How about your greatest fear?

Monica: Powerful technology falling into the wrong hands.

Gabriel: Do you have advice for the younger generations?

Monica: Um, enjoy it but don't depend on it.

Gabriel: Do you think your job is vulnerable to technology?

Monica: Yea, I think salespeople everywhere could be replaced.
